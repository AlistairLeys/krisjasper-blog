I wrote those numbers like a mongol. Anyways, 1 is for the House of Fraser bag, 2 is the chicken wrap and 3 is for the kitkat. Had it taken in Sainsbury's. The story behind the numbers? Here...

The other day, after going to the city centre to pay our bills I thought of going inside the House of Fraser to buy Martini glasses. So I walked in, went down the basement where the alcohols are located (thinking that the glasses for the drinks will be in there as well, as common sense will tell us). But Nah, when I asked an Eastern European lady where the glasswares are, she said in her hard Eastern European accent "6th floor". "Thank you" I said in my own Fil-Brit accent. But going to the 6th floor just to buy glasses was a challenge for me. That meant passing by the Men's section, and with them having sale that weekend it'd be hard to resist. But I fought it. While in the escalator I was thinking of all the clothes I bought last month that I still haven't worn. I thought of all the instances when I bought things that I resented later on coz I didn't really need them... I thought I'll go to the US within 8 weeks time so I need to save anyway.

So I settled buying one FCUK shirt. Then I went up to 6th floor.

As it was around 1pm, I thought should I go home, read, study and rest? Or should I just go to the Bullring (mall) as I was near it anyway. "A"'s at work for the day anyway... Home and study versus Mall and shop. Home or Mall? Study or shop?

So I settled studying what to shop for my incoming holidays.

So I went to Sainsbury's first, had chicken wrap for my lunch and saw that new kitkat for my dessert while on the go. The other bits in the basket are items we dont really need in the house like french mustard, olives, etc. But I bought them anyway coz I'm useless with budgeting.
And the rest of that day includes me and a number of till receipts, and MORE hard twanged Eastern European conversations coz they're everywhere now.

And regarding my previous post... Regarding the end bit.. You know, about that so called ghost story... That incident did not really happen. It's a BAD joke from me (in a way to prank you) in relation to April Fools' day.
ha, ha ..

Take care everyone.

updated (1915hrs)

Just got home.. The joke's on me. After having lunch, I renewed my membership in one shop and off I went to Marks and Spencer to buy anything I thought I'd fancy. Good thing I used the self check out machine coz it'll only accept cards. That's when I realized that I left my wallet in the last shop I visited. Inside my wallet were about £400, plus more than a dozen of debit and credit cards, and all my ID's including my nurse license and driving license. Anyone who'll keep it can steal my identity and will have access with my cards/banks. The ladies just smiled and said "you're very, very lucky" to me and handed me my wallet.

Thank GOD for good people. There are still a few left.

Speaking of few, I just watched TVPatrol, about an Aussie guy's blog against his Filipino boyfriend. Shame on that pinoy bf. I've visited this aussie's site once (you know, when I wander around and blog hop) but I just read one post and I thought it was'nt real, but hell... it's serious now eh? It's in TVP!!! I forget what's his site called now. Apparantly he gave him millions of pesos for an investment but that pinoy's keeping the money and the aussie wants it back. Lucky Fil bastard.
I hope he burns in hell.
